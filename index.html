Chemical Engineering Journal Advances · Volume 26, May 2026, 101132
Enhanced absorption performance of triethanolamine - bio-derived amino acid systems: Integrating experimental design and machine learning prediction
Bita Valadian, Peyvand Valeh-e-Sheyda, Amir Mehdi Parviz
DOI: 10.1016/j.ceja.2026.101132 Open access · Creative Commons
Research highlights: Novel bio-derived TEA-arginine green solvent for enhanced CO₂ capture • Threefold enhancement in mass transfer over MDEA benchmark (306 vs. 107 kmol/h·m³) • Gas flow rate identified as dominant operational parameter (61.7% contribution) • Random Forest model surpasses RSM in predictive accuracy (R² > 0.988) • Integration of green solvent design, process intensification, and machine learning.

Abstract: Triethanolamine (TEA) possesses high equilibrium capacity for CO₂ but is constrained by slow kinetics. This study introduces a novel hybrid solvent system synergistically combining TEA with bio-derived l-arginine (Arg) within a microfluidic contactor. The TEA-Arg system achieved a volumetric molar flux of 306 kmol/h·m³ (73% capture efficiency), a threefold enhancement over MDEA. Response surface methodology identified gas flow rate and arginine concentration as dominant factors (61.69% and 15.15%). Random Forest models accurately predicted key mass transfer parameters (R² > 0.988), enabling optimized CO₂ capture process design.
